The Filter and The Feeling

September 9, 2025 3 min read Katherine

Exploring the emotional impact of social media

As a Communications student, I spend a lot of time studying how we build our identities online. We learn to craft the perfect message, choose the most flattering photo, and present a curated version of our lives. This is our digital “highlight reel.”

But as a future psychology counselor, I’m drawn to what happens behind the screen. What is the emotional gap between the filtered photo and the real feeling? For many adolescents, this gap can feel like a canyon. The pressure to perform happiness online while navigating real-world struggles like anxiety or loneliness is immense.

"What we see is a single, polished frame, not the whole, beautifully complex picture."

My goal isn’t to condemn social media, but to understand this dynamic. How can we use these powerful communication tools to foster genuine connection instead of just comparison? It starts with acknowledging that what we see is a single, polished frame, not the whole, beautifully complex picture.
