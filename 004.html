The Power of a Purposeful Walk

September 10, 2025 3 min read Katherine

Finding peace through a purposeful walk

One of my simplest pleasures is taking a walk. It’s not about fitness or reaching a destination; it’s about creating mental space. In our hyper-connected world, our minds are rarely quiet. We jump from notifications to deadlines to social feeds, leaving little room for our thoughts to simply… settle.

I’ve found that a “purposeful walk”—where the only goal is to observe and listen, both to the world around me and my own inner voice—is a powerful tool for reasoning and reflection. It’s during these walks that tangled thoughts from an essay begin to straighten out, or I gain a new perspective on a problem.

"Sometimes, the most profound journey is the one you take inside your own mind."

For the young people I hope to work with one day, life can feel overwhelmingly loud. I believe that teaching them to find these small pockets of quiet, whether on a walk through a park or just by sitting with their thoughts for a few minutes, is a foundational skill for building mental resilience.
